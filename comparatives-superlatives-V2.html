Short Adjectives
The -er / -est pattern
🧠 The Syllable Rule

Count the syllables, not the letters

Short adjectives (1 syllable, sometimes 2) change shape. They don't need "more" or "most".

tall1 syllable
→
tall·ercomparative
→
tall·estsuperlative

Spelling patterns to remember

🔑 CVC Rule (consonant-vowel-consonant)

When to double the final letter

If a 1-syllable adjective ends in consonant + vowel + consonant (like big: b-i-g), double the last letter before adding -er / -est.

b-i-g → bigger / biggest
h-o-t → hotter / hottest
NOT: b-i-g → biger ✗

Long Adjectives
more / most — the helper words
📏 The Length Test

When a word is too long to change shape

Adjectives with 2+ syllables (except -y endings) use more and most as helpers. Think of them as carrying the comparison for the adjective.

beau·ti·ful3 syllables
→
more beautifulcomparative
→
the most beautifulsuperlative

Examples in context

BaseComparativeSuperlative
interestingmore interestingthe most interesting
expensivemore expensivethe most expensive
comfortablemore comfortablethe most comfortable
boringmore boringthe most boring
difficultmore difficultthe most difficult
famousmore famousthe most famous
⚠️ Critical Warning

NEVER combine both methods

✗ more taller / most tallest
✓ taller / the tallest

✗ more interestinger
✓ more interesting

You only ever use one method. Short adjectives change their ending. Long adjectives use helper words. Never both at the same time.

Common Errors
The ones you've been making — and why
🐛 Error Pattern 1

"more tall" / "more big"

She is more tall than him.

Why it happens: You learned "more + adjective" for long adjectives and applied it to short ones.

The fix: Tall has only 1 syllable → use -er form.

✗
more tall / more big / more fast
✓
taller / bigger / faster
🐛 Error Pattern 2

"interestinger" / "beautifuler"

This class is interestinger than math.

Why it happens: You applied the short-adjective rule to long words.

The fix: Interesting has 4 syllables → use more.

✗
interestinger / beautifuler / expensiver
✓
more interesting / more beautiful / more expensive
🐛 Error Pattern 3

"most tallest" / "most fastest"

He is the most tallest player on the team.

Why it happens: You used both methods at once — double marking.

The fix: Pick ONE method only. Never combine them.

✗
most tallest / more taller / most fastest
✓
the tallest / taller / the fastest
🐛 Error Pattern 4

Forgetting "the" in superlatives

Tokyo is largest city in Japan.

Why it happens: Articles are tricky in other languages.

The fix: Superlatives almost always need the.

✗
largest city / most beautiful place
✓
THE largest city / THE most beautiful place

Fix the Error

Rewrite the underlined error correctly.

My car is more fast than yours.
She is the most smartest student here.
