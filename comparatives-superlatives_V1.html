💡 Section 2

Comparative vs. Superlative

How many things are you comparing? That's the key question.

⚖️
Comparative
Comparing 2 things or people
A is ___ than B
Sam is taller than Alex.
Coffee is more expensive than tea.
🏆
Superlative
Comparing 3+ things — finding the extreme
A is the ___ of all
Sam is the tallest in the class.
Coffee is the most expensive drink.

beau·ti·ful (3) → more beautiful

🔵 Section 4

As … As — Equal Comparisons

Comparative and superlative aren't the only way to compare. When two things are equal, we use as…as — and it works the same for short AND long adjectives!

Subject + verb + as + adjective + as + noun/pronoun
✅ Works with ALL adjectives — short (1–2 syl.) and long (3+ syl.) — no change needed!
🔵 Short Adjectives (1–2 syllables)
tall My brother is as tall as my dad.
fast This car is as fast as that one.
big My room is as big as yours.
cold Today is as cold as yesterday.
old She is as old as my sister.
happy He looks as happy as a kid.
💡 KEY RULE
Short adjectives → NO -er. Just: as + adj + as
🟠 Long Adjectives (3+ syllables)
beautiful This city is as beautiful as Rome.
interesting That book is as interesting as this one.
expensive The hotel is as expensive as the flight.
comfortable This seat is as comfortable as that one.
important Sleep is as important as exercise.
difficult Math is as difficult as physics.
💡 KEY RULE
Long adjectives → NO "more". Just: as + adj + as
🔄 Negative Form — Not as … as
Subject + verb + not + as + adjective + as + noun/pronoun

🔵 Short: My cat is not as big as your dog. (= your dog is bigger)

🔵 Short: He doesn't run as fast as her. (= she is faster)

🟠 Long: This movie is not as interesting as that one. (= that one is more interesting)

🟠 Long: The exam was not as difficult as I expected. (= I expected it to be harder)

📊 Compare the Three Forms

MeaningShort adj. (tall)Long adj. (expensive)
A = B (equal) as tall as as expensive as
A > B (comparative) taller than more expensive than
A = extreme (superlative) the tallest the most expensive
A < B (negative) not as tall as not as expensive as

🚨 Section 5

Common Mistakes — Spotted!

These are the exact errors most students make. Let's fix them once and for all.

more tall
❌ "tall" has only 1 syllable → it's a SHORT adjective. Short adjectives use -er, not "more".
✅ taller
"Sam is taller than Alex."
more big
❌ "big" = 1 syllable → SHORT. Also remember: double the final consonant → bigg+er.
✅ bigger
"The bear is bigger than the dog."
interestinger
❌ "interesting" has 4 syllables → it's a LONG adjective. Long adjectives use more before them. Never add -er!
✅ more interesting
"This is more interesting than that."
most tallest
❌ DOUBLE superlative! You're using BOTH "most" AND "-est". Choose ONE: either the tallest OR the most tall (but the first is correct!).
✅ the tallest
"She is the tallest in the class."
more faster
❌ DOUBLE comparative! "faster" is already comparative. Adding "more" makes it a double error.
✅ faster
"The cheetah is faster than the horse."
gooder / goodest
❌ "good" is irregular! It doesn't follow any rule — you must memorize its special forms.
✅ better / the best
"This is better. That is the best!"

SHORT adj. (1–2 syllables)
the + adj + -EST

LONG adj. (3+ syllables)
the MOST + adj.
the most interesting · the most beautiful
STRUCTURE
A is the _____ of / in all
⚡ Irregular Forms
good → better → best
bad → worse → worst
far → farther → farthest
little → less → least
✏️ Spelling Rules
big → bigger (double consonant)
happy → happier (y → i)
nice → nicer (drop silent e)

as + adj (base form) + as

as taller as

most tallest
interestinger
